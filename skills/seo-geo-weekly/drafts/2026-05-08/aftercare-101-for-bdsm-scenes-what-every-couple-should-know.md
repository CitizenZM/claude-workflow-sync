<h2>The Psychology and Physiology of Aftercare</h2>
<p>Understanding what’s happening in your body and mind after a scene is key to understanding why aftercare is so important. The two most common post-scene phenomena are "sub drop" and "Dom drop."</p>

<h3>Understanding Sub Drop</h3>
<p>Sub drop is a psychological and emotional crash experienced by a submissive partner after a scene. During play, a submissive's body is often flooded with endorphins (natural painkillers and mood elevators) and adrenaline. When the scene ends, these chemical levels plummet, which can lead to feelings of sadness, anxiety, loneliness, irritability, or even shame. It’s a physiological response, not a reflection of the scene's quality or the partner's performance. Effective aftercare helps mitigate these effects by replacing the hormonal high with genuine comfort, reassurance, and connection.</p>

<h3>Recognizing Dom Drop</h3>
<p>Often overlooked, Dom drop is the equivalent experience for the Dominant partner. A Dominant expends significant mental and emotional energy maintaining control, making decisions, and ensuring the submissive's safety. They also experience an adrenaline rush. Afterward, they can be hit with feelings of guilt, self-doubt ("Did I go too far?"), emptiness, or a sudden sense of anti-climax. The responsibility of holding power can be heavy, and aftercare allows the Dominant to shed that role and receive care and validation themselves.</p>
